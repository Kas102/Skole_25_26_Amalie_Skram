Animals

India's state flower

The Lotus flower is the national flower of India. It holds a huge spiritually importance. Most of the Lotus flowers are either pink or white, but they can also be blue, purple and yellow. It is the national flower of India, because it symbolizes purity, beauty, wealth and spirituality. The plant is often known for its beauty.

India's state animal

The national animal of India is the Bengal tiger. The tiger is the largest in the cat family, and its known for its power and strength. Its colours are orange, white and black . The Bengal tiger lives for approximately eight to ten years in the wild.

Pygmy hog

The pygmy hog is an extremely rare animal. It is the smallest pig species in the world. They used to be widely spread all across the Himalayas, but nowadays they are only to be found in India. With their small height of only thirthy centimeters, they are a definite must see when visiting India.

Sangai deer

The Sangai deer is a critically endangered animal that can only be found at the Keibul Lamjao National Park. This animal is often referred to as the “dancing deer “. The name is given to them because of their weird movements, making them look like they are dancing .

Nilgiri tahr

The Nilgiri tahr is the only mountain ungulate that can be found in the southern part of India. It belongs to an endangered species. Amongst others the Nilgiri tahr faces threats such as deforestation and hunting for its meat and skin.
